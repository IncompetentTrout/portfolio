The Process

A project made with the collaboration of the games development and games programming course. This was to give an experience of working with a large group of people on one project imitating what it would be like to work at a large studio.

The class is split into their roles of: Design, Tech, and Art. A producer was selected to lead the project and helping them was the leads of each roles. Each of the lead would be in charge of organising and allocating tasks to their team whilst being in communication with the producer and the other leads. The responsibilities of each of the teams were as followed:

The design team crafted game mechanics and narrative concepts that were going to be in the game.
The art team were responsible for making the art and environment assets for the game. Due to the size of the art team, it had 2 leads and had different, smaller departments within that team that included 3D, 2D, and UI.
The tech team of making the mechanics and the functionality of the game in the chosen game engine of the producer.

My Role - Tech Lead

In my role as the Tech Lead, I was responsible for overseeing the tech team's activities, such as task allocation and deadline management. Providing technical direction for the project's development was crucial.
This position mirrors the responsibilities of a lead programmer in a game studio, involving supervising coding tasks, guiding the programming team, and ensuring that technical aspects align with the game's design.
Collaboration with other departments, issue troubleshooting, and maintaining code quality were also essential aspects of my role.
Overall, being the Tech Lead allowed me to exercise leadership and technical skills, ensuring the smooth progression of the project. It was a challenging yet rewarding experience that taught me valuable lessons in team management and technical problem-solving.

During the first week of this mini-module, the producer and team leads were selected to organise the members that selected their dicipline. After I was assigned as the tech lead, I met with my team to know who exactly was in my team, and what sort of mechanics they prefered to work on for the project. As this project has a high empasis on collaboration, I had also asked for the GitHub accounts of my team so that I could add them to the GitHub Project.

With that I compiled the roles and mechanics that will be in the project and assigned them to my team, organising and and scheduling when they should be done during the weeks we have for production.

After obtaining and adding all of the GitHub accounts of my team to be collaboratiors on the GitHub project, I put out instructions for my team on how to use Github in conjection with Unity for the collaborative work we were going to do on this project.

With this I was able to monitor what my team has done through the different branches of the GitHub project so far and to test if there were any bug that the mechanics made had or if the functionality of the mechanics fit the concept that the mechanics designers from the design team had for them.

I worked with the games mechanics designer to plan out the mechanics my team would make. We decided on the intricacies of the mechanics and whether certain functionalities are possible to be produced by the tech team.
What was discussed was then compiled onto a document for the tech team to follow.

For the second week of production, I continued to oversee the progress my team was making on the mechanics for this project. For the mechanics already made up to this point I bug tested them to ensure that they worked as the should and gave feedback on what to change or improve.
One such example was when I was play-testing the dash mechanic for the player. At that point, the dash could make the player dash through walls and would break the playability of the game.

Nearing the deadline of production, I was tasked by the producer to block out the levels for our playable beta for the assets and mechanics to be put upon.
